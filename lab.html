Red Blood Cells

Red blood cells (erythrocytes) are the oxygen carriers of your body. They contain hemoglobin, which binds oxygen in the lungs and releases it to tissues throughout your body.

Count: 4.5-5.5 million per microliter
Lifespan: Approximately 120 days
Size: 6-8 micrometers
Production: 2 million per second
Hemoglobin: ~270 million molecules per cell

White Blood Cells

White blood cells (leukocytes) are your body's defense force. They identify and destroy pathogens, produce antibodies, and maintain immune memory for future protection against diseases.

Count: 4,000-11,000 per microliter
Types: Neutrophils, Lymphocytes, Monocytes, Eosinophils, Basophils
Lifespan: Hours to years depending on type
Function: Immunity and defense
Response: Rapid mobilization to infection sites

Platelets

Platelets (thrombocytes) are small cell fragments that play crucial roles in blood clotting. They aggregate at injury sites to prevent blood loss and initiate the healing process.

Count: 150,000-450,000 per microliter
Lifespan: 8-10 days
Size: 2-3 micrometers
Function: Blood clotting and wound healing
Response time: Activated within seconds of injury

Plasma

Plasma is the liquid matrix of blood, comprising 55% of total blood volume. It transports nutrients, hormones, proteins, and waste products throughout the body while maintaining pH balance.

Composition: 90% water
Proteins: Albumin, globulins, fibrinogen
Contains: Electrolytes, nutrients, gases, hormones
pH: 7.35-7.45 (slightly alkaline)
Volume: Approximately 3 liters in adults
